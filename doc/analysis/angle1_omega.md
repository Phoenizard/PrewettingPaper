# Angle 1 — Wall affinity and the extent of prewetting

Topic: exploring the relation between the wall affinity of the solutes and the extent of the
prewetting regime in the $(\phi_{1,\infty}, \phi_{2,\infty})$ plane.

## Variables

Control variables

- $\omega_1$ — wall affinity of solute 1. More negative means stronger attraction of solute 1
  to the wall. It enters only $f_{surf}$, i.e. the wall boundary condition, and is independent
  of the bulk interactions $\chi_{ij}$.
- $\omega_2$ — wall affinity of solute 2, same convention and same role for solute 2.

Observables

Both are components of the extent of prewetting, measured in the $(\phi_{1,\infty}, \phi_{2,\infty})$
plane. They are complementary: one measures how far the prewetting line reaches, the other where
it sits.

- Length of the prewetting line — how wide a range of reservoir compositions triggers the
  thin-to-thick transition. This is the extent along the line: the longer the line, the more
  reservoir states at which prewetting occurs.
- Distance from the prewetting line to the binodal — how far from bulk coexistence the wall
  already induces a thick film. This is the extent transverse to the line: the larger the
  distance, the earlier (deeper into the one-phase region) the wall drives wetting.

Not measured here

The strength of the transition — the jump in surface excess $\Delta\Gamma$ between the thin and
the thick branch — is an independent dimension of prewetting. It cannot be read off a
two-dimensional phase map and is left out of this angle.
